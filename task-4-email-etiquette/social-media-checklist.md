The Social Media Responsibility Checklist

The Dos: Best Practices

* Do set your boundaries. Regularly check your privacy settings. Make sure personal photos or posts meant for friends aren't visible to everyone or future employers.

* Do build a brand. Use platforms like LinkedIn to share your projects, certificates and volunteer work. Make your online presence work for you.

* Do verify before you share. Take a minute to fact-check a post or news story before sharing it. Being a source builds your credibility.

* Do protect your accounts. Use passwords for each platform and turn on Two-Factor Authentication to prevent hackers.

* Do practice the Permanent Rule. Always assume that anything you post in a private group or a disappearing story can be saved forever.

The Don'ts: Common Pitfalls

* Don't share data. Never post photos of your college ID, Aadhaar card, flight boarding passes or internship offer letters. These have barcodes and details that thieves can use.

* Don't post when you're angry. Avoid venting about professors, group projects or internship experiences. Professional circles are smaller than you think.

* Don't share your location in time. It's safer to post photos after you've left a venue.

* Don't engage with trolls. Avoid arguments, in comment sections. Online hostility can reflect poorly on you.

* Don't trust Third-Party Apps. Never log in to apps using your media credentials; they might be used to collect your data.
